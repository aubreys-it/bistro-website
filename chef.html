ABOUT    EXECUTIVE CHEF BIO

Christopher Stallard

From kitchen to classroom and back to kitchen, Chef Christopher Stallard brings a life-long passion for learning which inspires Bistro by the Tracks' extraordinary, award-winning menu.

Christopher began his education at the University of Tennessee and eventually transfered to Johnson & Wales University in Charleston, SC where he graduated Summa Cum Laude having earned degrees in culinary arts and food service management. These degrees would afford Christopher the opportunity to eventually become a chef instructor at The Art Institute of Charleston where he taught American Cuisine, Garde Manger, Asian Cuisine, Art Culinaire and Dining Room Operations.

Christopher's regional culinary virtuosity is a product of over a decade of study around the southeast. During his tenure as Executive Chef at Knoxville’s Little Star Restaurant, he competed in the Sixth Annual Bacardi Recipe Classic where he took the Best of Show. Shortly thereafter, he joined The Orangery to assume the position of Executive Chef in 2006, and was awarded the AAA Four Diamond honor for the restaurant. Hungry for a new challenge, Christopher migrated back to Charleston for a six-year period where he would teach, at The Art Institute and grow as Chef de Cuisine at Tristan under chef Nate Whiting. After having learned from the best and sharing that knowledge with others, Christopher has returned to Knoxville as Executive Chef at Bistro by the Tracks.
